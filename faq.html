FIRE SAFETY FAQs

The questions we're asked most, on compliance, servicing, systems and costs. Can't find yours? Call, WhatsApp or send us a message and we'll answer within 24 hours.

Q·01Is a fire risk assessment a legal requirement in Kenya?+

Yes. Under Kenyan law, including the Occupational Safety and Health Act, 2007 and county government fire clearance requirements. Occupiers of workplaces and commercial premises must take measures to protect people from fire, which includes assessing fire risks. Our certified assessors carry out the survey and produce a written report accepted by regulators and insurers.

Q·02How often should fire extinguishers be serviced?+

Fire extinguishers should be professionally inspected and serviced at least once a year, with a basic visual check monthly. Extinguishers that have been discharged, damaged, or have lost pressure must be serviced immediately. We provide annual servicing, refilling and certification for all extinguisher types.

Q·03How many fire extinguishers does my building need?+

It depends on your floor area, building layout, occupancy and the fire risks present, a kitchen, server room and warehouse each need different coverage. As a general rule no point on a floor should be more than 30 metres from an extinguisher. We confirm exact numbers, types and positions during a free site assessment.

Q·04What is the difference between conventional and addressable fire alarm systems?+

A conventional system divides your building into zones, the panel tells you a zone has activated but not which device. An addressable system gives every detector and call point a unique address, so the panel shows the exact device and location that triggered. Conventional suits smaller buildings; addressable is ideal for large or complex premises.

Q·05Do you serve areas outside Nairobi?+

Yes. We are based in Nairobi and work across Kenya and East Africa. Free site assessments are available within Nairobi; for locations outside Nairobi, transport costs are discussed and agreed before any visit is booked.

Q·06How long does a fire safety installation take?+

A small office extinguisher and signage fit-out can be completed in a day. A full detection and alarm installation for a mid-sized building typically takes one to three weeks, while large or phased projects are scheduled around your operations. You receive a clear timeline with your formal quotation.

Q·07Do you offer maintenance contracts?+

Yes, we offer annual, bi-annual and quarterly maintenance contracts covering extinguishers, alarm panels, detectors, sprinklers, hose reels and emergency lighting. Contract clients receive scheduled service visits, full compliance documentation and priority callout.

Q·08Will I receive certificates for insurance and compliance?+

Yes. After every installation or service we issue the relevant certificates and service reports. These are accepted by insurers, regulators and county fire departments as evidence that your fire protection is professionally maintained.

Q·09What standards does your equipment comply with?+

We supply and install equipment built to recognised international standards, including BS EN 3 for portable extinguishers, BS EN 54 for fire detection and alarm components, and BS EN 12845 / NFPA 13 for sprinkler systems, alongside applicable Kenyan standards.

Q·10Do you provide fire safety training?+

Yes. We train your staff in fire prevention, correct extinguisher use, evacuation procedures and fire marshal duties. Training can be delivered at your premises and combined with a live extinguisher demonstration. Contact us to arrange a session for your team.
